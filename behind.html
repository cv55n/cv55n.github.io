i think about it all the time

this idea first came to me during a time where i was overwhelmed by my thoughts and started visualizing each one as an open tab. that shift in perspective helped me calm down and better manage myself. i also started to feel the mental heat and sluggishness, as if my brain is functioning like an overworked computer. after this moment i kept having recurring thoughts and started to imagine my mind in a computer context such as having opened thought tabs running in the background, memories surfacing at unwarranted times, elements of my inner world being organized into folders, and so on.

the human body and mind are so complex, yet beautiful. i felt compelled to dissect the mind by mapping it out using my macbook and tools available in ios. what started as a thought eventually turned into a passion project i've been working on since december 2024. the mind being exhibited does not belong to a real person, but it is influenced partly by my personal experiences. i chose not to model it after myself, as doing so felt too invasive to share publicly. instead, creating a fictional persona gave me the freedom to build a character with their own stories and inner turmoil, offering the audience a deeper look into this imagined mind. the process of making this was both healing and painful. i had to look inward and sit with vulnerable moments by really understanding and asking myself difficult questions to extract raw thoughts, emotions, experiences, and memories. i then translated those findings into my interpretation of what the brain would look like if it were modeled after a computer.

in this project, i focused on five categories: home screen, current, memories, you, and dreams.

the home screen is what i imagine the main starting screen to look like in someone's mind. you are welcomed with the main folders or the main characteristics that make up a person.

— within the folders there’s even more subfolders that go more in depth and it’s much more complex

the second screen displays a still image representing the current state of the person’s mind. we can see all the thoughts the character has had since waking up, along with some memories they’re reflecting on in the moment. a video recording accompanies the image, putting all of this into perspective by showing it happening in real time.

the memories folder holds all the memories but we can only see a portion. each memory has a key frame accompanied by a brief description. the folder also comes with its own system that helps better sort and organize memories more effectively.

the you screen shows when the character thinks of this person and in doing so she thinks of him as a whole.

the dream folder shows a small collection of recent dreams overlapping with old dreams. this is supposed to represent the fuzziness and blur memory you get when trying to recap a dream. you only remember parts of it and it all seems like a faint memory . in some cases it feels near impossible trying to recreate it in your head so you’re left with just fragments of still images.

there are some folders and parts of the brain i wished to have delved into and highlighted in this project but due to software limitation i was not able to do so. i hope in the near future i end up doing a second part that goes more in depth and is more detailed, but for now i'm happy with what i have created and excited i get to share this to the world.
